**Case Study**: The "Reverse Payment" UPI ScamUnderstanding the Threat

# Understanding the Threat:
Across India's digital landscape, UPI-based financial fraud has surged, primarily through a deceptive tactic where criminals manipulate users into authorizing outgoing payments under the guise of receiving funds. This specific scam exploits a common misunderstanding of how the Unified Payments Interface (UPI) functions, specifically regarding when a PIN is required.

**How the Scam Operates (Step-by-Step)**:-

**1. Initial Contact**: The fraudster calls or connects to victims offering more money than quoted for a specific item to buy or sell etc. They make a genuine looking fak eprofile and tell a condition like they have to move out quickly abroard to create a sense of urgency

**2. The Digital Trap**: Instead of a bank transfer, the scammer sends a QR code via a messaging app like WhatsApp, claiming falsely that the victim should scan the qr code to recieve payment.

**3. The Critical Mistake**: Though the truth is qr code and pin is entered only to send money. One should verify if its a pyment request or payment being made by themselves and not fall in the urgency trap by fraudsters by sharing their otp or secret-pin.

**4. Financial Drainage**: Once the PIN is submitted, the transaction is instantly authorized; rather than receiving funds, the victim's account is immediately debited, and the money vanishes into the criminal's account.

# Who is at Risk and the Impact?
People who are of old age, have lack of knowledge digital literacy and use digital payment system frequently. They are primary targets for socially engineered attacks as they get easily decieved by the fraudsters having no awareness of the frauds going on in the world. The consequences extend beyond the immediate loss of hard-earned money, victims often face significant psychological distress and the fear that their linked banking information has been permanently compromised
